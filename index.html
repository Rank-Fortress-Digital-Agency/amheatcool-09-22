How to Properly Maintain an Air Conditioning Unit


Summertime means hot, sticky weather. But it doesn’t have to mean discomfort thanks to your air conditioning unit. By following a few simple tips, you can keep your AC running smoothly all season long and prevent costly repairs. Here are some easy ways to maintain your AC unit:

Keep it clean

One of the most important things you can do to maintain your AC unit is to keep it clean. remove leaves, pollen, and other debris from the unit regularly. A build-up of these materials can hinder airflow and cause the unit to work harder than necessary. In addition, be sure to keep the area around the unit free of debris and vegetation.

Another important thing you can do to maintain your AC unit is to have it serviced by a professional regularly. This will ensure that the unit is running properly and that any potential problems are caught early. Servicing may also help improve the efficiency of the unit, which can save you money on your energy bills. Finally, be sure to follow the manufacturer’s instructions for the use and care of your AC unit. This will help ensure that it lasts for many years.

Change the filter


Another easy way to ensure your AC unit is running efficiently is to change the filter regularly. Depending on the type of filter you have, you may need to change it every few months or as often as once a month during peak usage times. A good rule of thumb is to change your filter at least once every three months.

In addition to changing your AC filter, you can also clean it every month. Simply remove the filter from its housing and gently vacuum away any dirt or debris that has collected on the surface. Be sure to reattach the filter securely before running your AC unit again.

Regular maintenance and cleaning of your AC unit will not only help it run more efficiently but will also prolong its lifespan. By taking these simple steps, you can keep your AC unit running smoothly for years to come.

Check for refrigerant leaks


Freon is what helps cool the air inside your home and if there’s a leak, it can cause your unit to work harder than necessary to cool the air. If you think there may be a leak, contact a professional AC repair person to check it out and make any necessary repairs.

If your Freon levels are low, it could be because of a leak, or because the Freon needs to be recharged. Either way, it’s best to have a professional AC repair person take a look at it to make sure everything is working properly.

Keep vents clear

For your AC unit to work properly, the vents in your home need to be clear. Be sure to vacuum or dust around them regularly so that air can flow freely. Also, be sure to keep any branches or other objects from blocking the vents. This could cause your AC unit to work harder than necessary and use more energy, costing you more money on your utility bills.

If you want to save money on your air conditioning bill, make sure to close the blinds or curtains in rooms that get a lot of suns. The sun emits a lot of heat, which can cause your room to become very warm. Closing the blinds will help block out some of the sun’s heat so that your room doesn’t get too hot. Schedule annual maintenance


Just like your car needs an oil change now and then, your AC unit needs some annual maintenance as well. This includes things like cleaning the coils and drains and checking for Freon leaks. By having a professional do this yearly maintenance, you can prevent larger problems down the road. Not to mention, it will keep your AC unit running more efficiently, which will save you money on your energy bills.

If you live in an area that gets very hot during the summer, then you know how important it is to have a working air conditioner. But did you know that there are some things you can do to help keep your AC unit running properly? With a little bit of preventative maintenance, you can avoid costly repairs or even replacements.

Keep the surrounding area clear


It’s important to keep the area around your AC unit free from debris and clutter. This includes things like lawn furniture, toys, and tools. Not only can these items interfere with airflow, but they can also be a potential safety hazard if they were to fall on the unit. Be sure to also regularly check and clean the unit’s filters. A build-up of dirt and debris can cause the unit to work harder than it needs to, which can lead to premature wear and tear.

In addition, it’s a good idea to have your AC unit serviced by a professional at least once a year. This will help ensure that it’s running efficiently and catch any potential problems before they become serious.

Invest in a good cover


When winter rolls around, it’s important to protect your AC unit from the cold weather. A good cover will help keep snow and ice off of the unit and will also protect it from any debris that may blow around.

It’s also a good idea to have your AC unit serviced before the winter months hit. This will help ensure that it’s running properly and will help prevent any issues from occurring during the colder months. If you live in an area where snow and ice are common, it’s also important to invest in a quality snow blower. This will make clearing your unit much easier and will also help prevent any damage that may occur from the heavy snowfall.

By following these simple tips, you can keep your AC unit running smoothly all season long. Not only will this save you money on repairs, but it will also help keep your home comfortable during the hot summer months.
